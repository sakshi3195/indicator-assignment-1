Crime Type Categories: Definition & Description

The information retrieved using CLEARMAP reflects incidents reported in Chicago, Illinois. This data reflects incidents where the police responded and completed case reports. It should be noted that though the police responded to an incident, a case report may not be generated. Also, this information does not reflect 911 calls for service. The case crime type classification is based upon information known at the time the preliminary investigation was conducted. It may be revised at a later time.

Crime classification codes are derived from the Federal Bureau of Investigation's (FBI) National Incident-Based Reporting System (NIBRS). Uniform Crime Reporting (UCR) Program. Information regarding the NIBRS & UCR Program may be accessed on-line at www.fbi.gov.

The crime classifications, which follow, have been summarized due to the often lengthy and complex nature of the crimes. These definitions are not verbatim, may not include every element of every offense, and should not be used in a court of law or for legal reference. They are only basic attempts to describe primary classifications. For thorough and accurate definitions of the law or interpretations of the law, users should refer to Illinois Compiled Statutes at www.ilga.gov/.

City of Chicago Municipal Code (MCC) information (laws enacted for the City of Chicago) is available on-line at:www.chicityclerk.com/legislation/codes/index.html.
